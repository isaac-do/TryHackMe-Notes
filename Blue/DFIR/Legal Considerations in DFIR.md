# Incident Detection and Initial Response
---
## Documentation
### *What happened.* 
Key points to document:
- Details of person of interest (POI), someone being investigated or questioned, and their involvement in the incident.
- The specific action that led to the incident.
- Nature and extent of the data compromised or lost.
- Any direct or indirect evidence that will help shed light on the incident.

Absence of Evidence is not Evidence of Absence

### *When it happened.* 
Important timestamps to record:
- Exact time and date the incident was detected and believed to occur. Ideally, logged in a standard format like UTC.
- Timeline of events leading up to the detection.
- Response time and actions are taken immediately following detection.

### Why it happened and how to prevent.
Important points to include:
- Analysis of the cause.
- Identifying and documenting gaps is knowledge, communication, or technical defenses that the incident highlighted.
- Formulating recommendations for policy updates, training enhancements, and technical measures to prevent recurrence.
